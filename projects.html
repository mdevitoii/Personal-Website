Schedule Manager

Java Program with Real-World Applications

What do I do when I find myself stumped while making a floor plan at my job? Make a Java program to help me! This program, once it is complete, will store all my employees, their preferred or best positions, and their hours of work during shifts I lead. Then, the program will create a floor plan based on the number of employees I have on clock, the number of positions that need to be filled for the hour, and how busy our previous hours have been. This program is a personal project that stemmed from my Programming II course, where I am learning the aspects of Object-Oriented Programming in Java.

Homelab Upgrades

Self-Hosted Wikipedia?!

I've been hard at work with my home lab for the past few months. After installing two new 2TB drives for media storage, I was stumped on what I should focus on next. Of course, I still needed to use PhotoPrism to organize family photos for Plex, but I wanted a new challenge. That’s when I stumbled upon a video tutorial for setting up Kiwix, which can be used to self-host your own instance of any wiki, including the infamous Wikipedia! Who knew that the entirety of human knowledge, hosted on a massive platform like Wikipedia in English, would only be about 90GB in size? I decided to self-host my own instance of Wikipedia to show my competency with Docker, as well as to show off what a home server can do!
